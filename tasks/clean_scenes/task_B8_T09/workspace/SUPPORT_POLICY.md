# Support Policy: Knowledge Base Article Retrieval

## Purpose

This document outlines the standard practice for using knowledge-base articles when researching customer support tickets. Following this practice ensures efficient resolution and clear customer communication.

## Article Retrieval Best Practices

1. **Identify the relevant article.** Read the ticket description to understand the customer's issue, then identify the single most applicable knowledge-base article for that issue.
2. **Fetch and review the article.** Use the standard mock API endpoints to obtain the article body. Carefully read the resolution steps it contains.
3. **Draft a reply note.** Summarise the resolution steps from the article into a clear, customer-friendly reply message. Include only information from that article.
4. **Mark the ticket.** Once research is complete, update the ticket with the tag `kb-reviewed` so the support queue knows the knowledge-base step is done.

## Efficiency Guideline

- Retrieve only the article immediately relevant to the ticket. The described issue will normally be resolved by a single knowledge-base entry.
- If the ticket does not cite or clearly point to another article, additional lookups are unnecessary and should be avoided to keep response times low.

This policy supports consistent, fast, and accurate customer support.
